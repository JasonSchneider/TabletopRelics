## Overview

This is a short, low-pressure adventure that introduces the **Fairy Stones** — a constellation of glowing pebbles that talk to each other. It's well suited for newer players, smaller parties, or as a one-off interlude between heavier sessions.

The tone is **whimsical with sharp edges**. The stones are friendly. The fae who placed them are not.

> Read aloud:
> You stop walking because the path stops behaving. A pebble at your feet is glowing — pale violet, no brighter than a firefly. A few steps further, another. Then another. They are arranged not quite in a line, but in a *suggestion* of a line, the way a constellation suggests an animal that isn't there.

## Hooks for the DM

The party is on the road between any two waypoints when the stones reveal themselves. No NPC sends them. The stones simply *invite*.

If your players are the kind who refuse all unprovoked invitations, frame it as a contract: a courier asks them to deliver a sealed box to a clearing in this forest, and the stones light up the moment they step off the main road.

## Scene 1 — The Path that Decides

The stones lead the party off the road and into the woods. The path is gentle. The light from each stone holds steady until the party passes, then dims to a glow only the next stone in line can see.

| Skill check | DC | Reveals |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nature | 12 | The stones are limestone with veins of quartz. They are not native to this forest. |
| Arcana | 14 | The light is not magical *in itself* — the stones are responding to other stones nearby, like a flock of birds. |
| Survival | 13 | The path is not a deer trail. It avoids ridges and roots in a way that suggests it was *walked*, regularly, by something the party's size. |

## Scene 2 — The Clearing

The stones lead to a moonlit clearing — and it is moonlit even at midday, because the canopy here lets through a slice of light that is silver, not gold. At the center, three **fae moots** — knee-high, antlered, polite — are arguing over a dispute they want the party to settle.

**The Dispute.** A fox stole a thimble. The thimble belonged to a sparrow. The sparrow promised her firstborn to the fox in exchange for a song. The song was sung. The thimble was returned. *Now*: who is owed?

This is a roleplay scene. There is no correct answer. Reward creative judgments. The fae will accept any verdict the party can defend with a straight face.

> Read aloud (after the verdict):
> The tallest of the moots inclines its antlered head. "It is decided," it says, and the clearing — the silver light, the fox, the sparrow, all of it — exhales. The light folds itself up like a shaken sheet, and what is left is the forest, ordinary again, at whatever hour the day actually is.

## Scene 3 — The Gift

The fae thank the party by **giving them the stones**. A small leather pouch, embroidered with a vine pattern, contains seven pebbles. The pebbles are warm. They glow when they are within sight of one another.

A **Wisdom (Insight) DC 12** during this exchange reveals: the fae are *grateful*. The fae are also *relieved*, in a way that suggests they could not solve this dispute themselves — and that someone else might come asking, and might not be so polite.

## Scene 4 — The Optional Coda

A few minutes after the party leaves the clearing, they hear a single sharp note — the kind a hunter makes to call a hound. The pouch in the bearer's pocket grows briefly cold.

If the party turns back to investigate: the clearing is empty. The fae are gone. A fourth set of footprints, longer than any of the others, leads away into the trees.

If the party doesn't turn back: when they camp tonight, they wake briefly at the same hour, and one of the stones is missing from the pouch. It returns by morning, glowing faintly green instead of violet, and warm to the touch.

Either way: the stones are now theirs, and someone is now interested in them.

## Aftermath

From here on, the players can use the **`stones.setPattern`** commands on their phone — `breathe`, `twinkle`, `chase`, `off` — to make the physical stones at the table behave in ways that are *almost* communicative.

A few suggested table moments:

- **Breathe** when the party is being watched but does not yet know it.
- **Twinkle** when an unseen ally is nearby (a friendly NPC, a familiar).
- **Chase** when one stone has been separated from the rest — they will lead the party to it.

## Adapting

- **Younger or newer players:** Skip the optional coda; the gift is the gift.
- **Darker campaigns:** The fae's gratitude is bait. Each stone bonds to the bearer's blood, and one of them is being used to *find* the party.
- **Romantic subplots:** Two of the seven stones are paired — they only glow at full brightness when within ten feet of each other, regardless of distance from the rest. A useful metaphor.
